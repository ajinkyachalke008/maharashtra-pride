⚠ Critical Intelligence Findings
CRITICAL
F-01 · Digital Arrest Scam — ₹56,18,206 Extracted via Police Impersonation
The complainant was contacted on an unregistered number (7545036749) by a caller posing as Mumbai Cyber Police, who staged a uniformed video call, displayed a fabricated Aadhaar and Canara Bank debit card, and alleged the victim's identity was used to launder ₹2 Cr with a ₹20L "commission" credited to them. Under threat of arrest and instructed to remain home and report every 2 hours, the victim broke 2 fixed deposits and transferred funds in two tranches (04/04 and 05/05/2026) totalling ₹56,18,206 to two mule accounts, believing the money would be returned after "RBI verification."

CRITICAL
F-02 · Two Mule Hubs Receive Funds Under Shell Business Names
Both primary mule accounts were registered under business names rather than individuals: Axis Bank A/C 925020057462361 (Nova Next Mart Pvt Ltd, IFSC UTIB0000026) received ₹22,18,206, and IndusInd Bank A/C 258295299526 (Rilex Enterprises, IFSC INDB0001915) received ₹34,00,000 — the latter partly funded by gold pledged at a Manipuram Gold branch in Baleli Phata, Pune. Both accounts were drained to near-zero balances (residual holds of ₹0.12–₹660) within days, confirming they exist purely as pass-through shells.

F-03 · Layer-2 Fan-out Spreads Funds Across 15 Unrelated Bank Accounts
Within hours, the two mule hubs split funds across 15 distinct accounts at 12 different banks/FIs — including Central Bank of India, Fino Payments Bank, PNB, Yes Bank, NSDL Payments Bank, SBI, Kerala Gramin Bank, State Bank of Mauritius, IDFC First, Federal Bank, Indian Overseas Bank, and a second Axis Bank account receiving the single largest hop of ₹11,50,000. Several recipients are named individuals (Vanshika Gupta, Narayan Lal Damami, Rashi Karamchandani, Ajit Singh, Mamta, Muhammed Junaid K) — likely mule account holders recruited for a fee.

F-04 · Organized Mule-Wallet Cluster in Hardoi, Uttar Pradesh
A cluster of six near-identical Jio Payments Bank wallets (account series 3321721xxxxxx) each received ₹9,000–₹10,000 and were independently cashed out via ATM at the same four locations in Hardoi district — Pihani Branch, SBI Cinema Chauraha, Hardoi Main Branch, and ADB Hardoi — totalling ₹1,80,000 across 18 withdrawals. F-06 · Physical Cash-Out Trail Spans 4 States — High-Value Investigative Leads
Five cheque encashments (₹20,50,800 total) occurred at named branches in New Delhi, Kollam (Kerala), Jalandhar (Punjab) and Pali (Rajasthan), each with a named branch manager and contact number on file. F-07 · Inflow/Outflow Imbalance at Both Mule Hubs — Possible Co-mingled or Unlinked Funds
Reconciling actual debits against credits at the two Layer-1 hubs reveals a discrepancy worth flagging rather than glossing over: Axis Bank A/C ...462361 received ₹22,18,206 from the victim but only ₹19,73,000 is traced onward across its 7 outbound transactions — a shortfall of ₹2,45,206 not accounted for in this dataset (likely a direct cash withdrawal at source, or balance currently sitting frozen — needs the bank's full statement to confirm). Conversely, IndusInd Bank A/C ...9526 disperses ₹38,99,340 across 8 outbound transactions despite receiving only ₹34,00,000 from the victim — an excess of ₹4,99,340, meaning either this account holds co-mingled legitimate business funds (it is registered to "Rilex Enterprises," an active business name, not a shell), or it received additional fraud proceeds from a different, currently unlinked victim. Both possibilities warrant direct verification with the respective banks before the pilot demo cites these figures as final.
